# Observations from winning the love of a small, bear-like creature

An unreasonable amount of my confidence comes from winning the love of this small bear-like creature:

[]

You see, when we met, she decided that I was the devil. Every time I stood up she'd bark at me. Every time I tried to pet her, she'd jump away as if I was about to hit her. She despised me.

She was generally a very shaky trauamatised creature back then but her hatred manifested particularly when it came to me:

[]

In a normal world, we would've gone our seperate ways, but we were now bound together by our mutual love for my now wife - so we had to make it work.

When a human shows hatred towards you, it's understandable to want to reciprocate - even though their hatred mostly has nothing to do with - but with dogs, that reaction is clearly makes no sense.

Unlike humans, dogs lack an episodic memory - meaning their emotional reactions are often just patterns overlayed on their experience that they can't rationalise away. 

A human can think "I'm afraid of him because he reminds me of that man" but a dog just expeirences that feeling without understanding why.

For Sam - aka Snarf - I clearly pattern-matched onto something horrible in her mind. 

I couldn't tell her what a great guy I was, I just had to show her. 

So that's what I try to do. 

My wife who raised street dogs told me it would be like melting an ice-cube. 

How hard could that be?
